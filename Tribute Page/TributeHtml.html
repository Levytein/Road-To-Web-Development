  • 1986 -Toriyama's manga was adapted and divided into two anime series that were produced by Toei Animation: Dragon Ball and Dragon Ball Z broadcasting in Japan from 1986-1996. During DragonBall's serialization, Torishima recruited him to work as a character designer for the 1986 role-playing video game
    Dragon Quest.
    The basic premise of most Dragon Quest games is to play a hero (actually named "Hero" in spinoff fiction, but in some games, the player is able to name their hero) who is out to save the land from peril at the hands of a powerful evil enemy, with the hero usually accompanied by a group of party members. Common elements persist throughout the series and its spinoff games: turn-based combat; recurring monsters, including the Slime, which became the series' mascot; a text-based menu system; and random encounters in most of the main series.
  • 1989 - Dragon Ball Z, the sequel to Dragon Ball, premiered on Fuji Television on April 26,1989, which ran for 291 episodes until its conclusion on January 31,1996.

  • 2002 - Season Three of Dragon Ball Z premiered on Cartoon Network and was the highest-rated program ever at the time on Cartoon Network. In the week ending in September 22, Dragon Ball Z was the #1 program of the week on all of television with boys 9-14 and men 12-24.
